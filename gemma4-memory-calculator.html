Configuration

Model variant
PLE (Per-Layer Embeddings) cannot be 4-bit quantized — it stays fp16 and dominates static footprint. E2B saves ~3.5 GB on this line alone, at the cost of model capability.
Weight quantization
4-bit cuts the linear-weights column ~4× vs fp16. Adds a bnb dequant kernel per forward pass (~800 MB transient scratch on E4B). Negligible quality loss for SFT tasks.
Training mode
Full FT trains all ~8B params — massive gradients and optimizer state (30-50 GB just for that). LoRA trains 0.1-1% of params. Practically required to fit Gemma 4 on a single GPU.

Source & assumptions

Formulas calibrated empirically against the OOM events in the OOM mitigations doc (T4 ~10 GB static, L4 ~22 GB at step 2, A100 ~17 GB at step 0). Naive textbook estimates predict ~13 GB, but real measurements show 17-22 GB. Why the gap:

  • NF4: 0.55 bytes/param (4-bit code + per-block fp16 scale)
  • Embeddings: vocab × hidden × 2 bytes (no quantization; PLE adds ~3.5B more fp16 params on E4B)
  • LoRA: r × (in+out) per projection × layers, fp16
  • adamw_8bit: ~6 bytes/trainable param (m+v 8-bit + fp32 master)
  • paged_adamw_8bit: ~4 bytes/trainable param (master paged to CPU)
  • Activations: bs × seq × (hidden + mlp_intermediate) × layers × 6, ÷ 4 under Unsloth checkpointing — Unsloth's checkpointing keeps attn-block intermediates, so the reduction is ~4× not 8×. Per-layer holds residuals, layer norms, Q/K/V, attention scores, MLP gate/up/down intermediates.
  • bnb dequant scratch (accumulated): per-tile size × 3 — multiple layers' dequant'd fp16 tiles coexist in PyTorch's caching allocator. Sequence-independent, so dropping seq does NOT help this.
  • Cross-entropy logits + backward: bs × seq × vocab × 2 × 3 — Gemma 4's 262k vocab makes this dominant on L4. The ×3 covers forward logits + grad-w.r.t.-logits + log_softmax intermediates. This is the line that pushes L4 over 22 GB even with sort + checkpointing on.
  • cuBLAS + autocast workspaces: ~2.5 GB — the LM head matmul over 262k vocab needs larger cuBLAS scratch than typical hidden-to-hidden ops; bf16 autocast also keeps brief fp32 intermediates for stability (softmax, layer norm).
  • Framework overhead: 1.5 GB with expandable_segments, 2.5 GB without — allocator fragmentation, PEFT/Unsloth wrapper allocations, autograd graph storage.
